About Out There Exeter

Out There Exeter was born from the desire for getting out there and celebrating inclusivity and queer culture. We ignite networks, strengthen connections, and celebrate inclusivity within our vibrant LGBTQIA+ community. We are reimagining Exeter's queer landscape by empowering voices, out there, to come forward and tell their stories.

Igniting Networks for LGBTQI+ people to connect and build a powerful force for equality and social inclusion.

Strengthening Communities means building safe spaces and fostering connections that uplift and empower each other.

Celebrating Inclusivity welcomes all to be their authentic selves, to belong, and to shine brightly.

Out There Exeter are dedicated to uplifting and celebrating what's out there in Exeter's LGBTQIA+ community through arts, culture, sports and advocacy.

Out There Queer Fest, a 14-day queer fringe-style festival (September 23rd - October 6th 2024), bridges the gap between artists, communities, netowrks, and individuals within the LGBTQIA+ sphere.

Out There Queer Fest

Get ready to celebrate all things queer. Out There Exeter are excited to deliver a festival of queer culture, an explosion of creative arts, sporting trimphs, advocacy and pure joy for LGBTQIA+ people and friends.

We envision Out There Queer Fest as an annual national event, making Exeter a cultural hub akin to Edinburgh Fringe Festival.

We want to introduce a varied and wide-reaching programme, strategically centring the inaugural festival on the LGBTQIA+ community, building on Exeter's strong queer community, society groups, and arts scene. This year's festival and events are supported by core Exeter venues: Exeter Phoenix, Exeter Northcott Theatre, Barnfield Theatre, Royal Albert Memorial Museum, and Exeter Library.


Queer Culture

Out There Exeter recognise the significance of queer culture to the LGBTQIA+ community out there. Out There Queer Fest exists to celebrate queer culture and to challenge the status quo by making queer culture more accessible and equitable.

Queer culture encompasses the diverse and vibrant experiences of LGBTQIA+ individuals. It's a tapestry woven from threads of self-expression, creativity, community, activism, and resilience.
